Chinstrap penguin

The diet of the chinstrap penguin consists of krill, shrimp, fish, and squid which they swim up to 80 km offshore each day to obtain.

  • Scientific Name: Pygoscelis antarcticus
  • Body Length: 72 cm
  • Weight: 3-5 kg
  • Lifestyle: Colony

The chinstrap penguin is a species of penguin which inhabits a variety of islands and shores in the Southern Pacific and the Antarctic Ocean. Its name derives from the narrow black band under its head which makes it appear as if it were wearing a black helmet, making it one of the most easily identified types of penguin.
